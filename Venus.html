Earth's Twin

Venus got its name after the roman goddess of love and has often been referred to as Earth's twin. It has also gone by both the morning star and the evening star because it can only be seen near sunrise and sunset. Venus is the closest planet to Earth and also lies within the goldilocks zone, meaning that at a time it was possible for Venus to contain life. It takes longer for one day to happen on Venus than a year with a day lasting 243 Earth days while a year is only 225 Earth days. The surface of Venus is the hottest of any planet in the solar system at more than 860 degrees Fahrenheit which is hot enough to melt lead. This is caused by the thick atmosphere Venus has.
