Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun in our solar system and is also the smallest. It's a rocky planet with a diameter of about 4,880 kilometers.

Mercury's surface is covered with craters, and it has a very thin atmosphere consisting mainly of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen. Due to its lack of a substantial atmosphere, Mercury experiences extreme temperature variations between day and night.

To learn more about Mercury and its features, you can visit the NASA Mercury overview page.
